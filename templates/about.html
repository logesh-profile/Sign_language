The Challenge

The Communication
Gap Is Real

Over 430 million people worldwide live with disabling hearing loss. Sign language is their primary language — yet the hearing world speaks a different one. The result is an invisible wall: at hospitals, in classrooms, and across workplaces, critical conversations are lost.

Professional interpreters are scarce, expensive, and unavailable around the clock. Technology until now has only offered basic gesture-to-letter mapping — not real conversation. SignaVerse changes that.

How It Works

The AI Pipeline

Four precision stages — from camera feed to spoken word — running in a seamless, real-time loop.

🤚
Step 01

Hand Gesture Capture

OpenCV + MediaPipe extract 21 hand landmarks from your webcam at 30fps — tracking every finger joint in real time.

🧠
Step 02

Deep Learning Recognition

A custom CNN model classifies gesture sequences against a trained library of 94%+ accuracy sign patterns.

📝
Step 03

Sentence Formation Engine

Detected signs are sequenced, stabilized via majority-vote buffer, and assembled into full sentences.

🔊
Step 04

Natural Speech Output

The formed sentence is converted into expressive, natural-sounding voice — delivered in <200ms.
